### Ingredients:
- 1 whole rotisserie chicken, shredded
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 2–3 carrots, sliced
- 3–4 stalks celery, sliced
- 3–4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter
- 10 cups water
- 5 chicken bouillon cubes
- Pinch of pepper, to taste
- Dried thyme, to taste
- Dried parsley, to taste
- 4–5 oz wide egg noodles (about ⅓ of a 12 oz bag — they expand a lot, save the rest)

### Instructions:
#### Build the Base:
1. Melt butter in a large pot over medium heat.
2. Add onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté until softened, about 10–15 minutes — carrots take longer than you'd expect.
3. While they cook, mince the garlic. Add it to the pot and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
4. While the garlic cooks, start shredding the rotisserie chicken (or shred it before you start cooking so it's ready to go).

#### Make the Broth:
1. Add 10 cups water and 5 bouillon cubes. Stir until dissolved.
2. Add thyme, parsley, and pepper to taste.

#### Finish the Soup:
1. Add shredded chicken and stir to combine.
2. Bring to a simmer.
3. Add noodles and cook 6–8 minutes until tender.
4. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve hot.

*~30–40 minutes total*
