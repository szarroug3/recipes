### Source:
[The Woks of Life](https://thewoksoflife.com/mongolian-chicken/)

### Ingredients:
- 12 oz boneless skinless chicken breast or thighs, patted dry and cut into 1/4-inch thick slices
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil (plus 1/3 cup for frying)
- 1/4 cup cornstarch (plus 2 tbsp for slurry)
- 1 tsp ginger, julienned or minced
- 5 dried red chili peppers (optional)
- 2 cloves garlic, chopped
- 3 scallions, cut diagonally into 1-2 inch slices
- 2 1/2 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp brown sugar
- 1/4 cup hot water or low sodium chicken stock
- 2 tbsp water (for cornstarch slurry)

### Instructions:
#### Prepare Chicken:
1. Mix sliced chicken with 1 tbsp oil.
2. Dredge chicken in 1/4 cup cornstarch, shaking off the excess.

#### Fry Chicken:
1. Heat 1/3 cup oil in a wok over high heat.
2. Re-dredge chicken if needed, then spread pieces evenly in the wok.
3. Fry for 1 minute, or until browned and crispy.
4. Flip and fry the other side for 30 seconds until crispy.
5. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate.

#### Make Sauce:
1. Drain oil from wok, leaving 1 tbsp. Heat over medium-high.
2. Add ginger and fry for 20 seconds.
3. Add chili peppers, garlic, and the white portions of the scallions. Stir-fry for 15 seconds.
4. Add soy sauce, brown sugar, and water or stock. Simmer until sugar dissolves.
5. Let sauce simmer for 2 minutes.
6. Mix 2 tbsp cornstarch with 2 tbsp water to make a slurry. Slowly stir slurry into sauce until it coats the back of a spoon.

#### Finish & Serve:
1. Add chicken and the green scallion portions to the wok. Toss for 10 seconds.
2. The sauce should cling to the chicken with almost no liquid remaining. Adjust consistency if needed.
3. Serve over jasmine rice.
