This often-copied–never-duplicated recipe was created in the fall of 1985.
Ginger root, chilies and garlic give it a distinctive, spicy taste
which some people call addictive!

It has been featured in Barbara Williams’ “Coasting & Cooking Cookbook,”
along with recipes from the finest restaurants on the Washington Coast.

### Ingredients:
- 1 oz. fresh ginger root, scrubbed and chunked
- 2 cloves garlic
- 1 tsp. crushed chili peppers
- 5 1/4 - 7 1/4 cups canned or fresh diced tomatoes
- 1 3/4 cups dry roasted unsalted peanuts
- 1 medium onion, chopped
- 1 1/2 cups chicken stock
- 3 cups water
- 1/4 cup flour
- 1/4 cup butter, melted
- 1/2 lb. cooked and cubed turkey or chicken

Note: For vegetarian option, leave out turkey or chicken and use vegetable stock instead of chicken stock.

### Instructions:
1. Blend in food processor:
   - 1 oz. fresh ginger root, scrubbed and chunked
   - 2 cloves garlic
   - 1 tsp. crushed chili peppers
2. Add to processor and chop, leaving chunky, then add to soup pot:
   - 3 1/4 cups canned or fresh diced tomatoes
   - 1 3/4 cups dry roasted unsalted peanuts
   - 1 medium onion, chopped
3. Add and cook to 165°:
   - 1 1/2 cups chicken stock
   - 3 cups water
4. Make a Roux paste. Add to thicken:
   - 1/4 cup flour mixed into 1/4 cup melted butter
5. Finally, add:
   - 2–4 cups diced tomatoes, canned or fresh
   - 1/2 lb. cooked and cubed turkey or chicken
6. Whisk warm roux into soup and simmer to thicken.
7. Heat to 160°. Add final tomatoes to thin and to add chunkiness to soup.
8. Reduce heat to 145° to serve.
9. Garnish with peanuts. Serves 6–8.
